About

Building the identity layer
for the AI era.

Jay J. Springpeace's intellectual journey began with Computer Engineering studies, followed by advanced research at Harvard University. He later lectured at CASYS — the Centre for Hyperincursion and Anticipation in Ordered Systems (CHAOS asbl) at the University of Liège, Belgium — one of the world's most distinctive AI research environments.

CASYS, founded by Prof. Daniel M. Dubois, pioneered the theory of anticipatory computing systems — AI systems that compute their current state by modeling and anticipating future states, not just reacting to the past. This fundamentally different approach to intelligence — incursion, hyperincursion, emergent autonomy — shaped Springpeace's conviction that AI systems need identity, continuity, and accountability to function responsibly in the real world.

While mainstream AI followed the statistical deep learning path, the CASYS community in Liège was asking different questions: Can a system anticipate? Can it carry responsibility across time? Can it have identity?

Those questions, first explored in Belgium in the late 1990s and early 2000s, became the philosophical and technical foundation for what is now the AIBSN Registry.
